They called it "restructuring." A euphemism for the inevitable. The fluorescent lights in the break room flickered as the announcement was made. Faces fell, eyes dropped. Some cried, others stared blankly. The room was silent, except for the hum of the air conditioning.

It was a Wednesday. The same day every week. The day the budget meetings took place. The day decisions were made, lives altered. A spreadsheet optimization exercise. A resource allocation strategy. Euphemisms for who gets to keep their job, who doesn't.

The ones who stayed worked longer hours, took on more responsibilities. Their eyes, too, lost their sparkle. They nodded, smiled, and complied. The ones who left packed up their things, quietly exited, and disappeared. No fanfare, no ceremony. Just erasure.

Their families received a letter, a bland, templated apology. A severance package, a weak attempt to salve the wound. The outplacement services, a cruel joke. A one-way ticket to the next precarious gig, the next layoff.

The company rebranded, rebounded, and renamed. The cycle repeated. Another town, another factory, another round of "restructuring." The faces changed, but the story remained the same. The powerful decided, the powerless complied. Lives were torn apart, rearranged, and discarded.

The echoes of those who got fucked lingered. In the abandoned buildings, in the rusting machinery, in the empty streets. A weight settled, like dust, like ash. It accumulated, quietly, relentlessly.
