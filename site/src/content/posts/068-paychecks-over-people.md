title: "Paychecks Over People"
number: 68
date: "2026-07-10"

Someone leaves. The rest get told it's "restructuring." Their work doesn't change, but their pay does. Overnight, priorities shift. Projects they poured their time into get reassigned or shelved. 

The ones who stay are told to be grateful for their jobs. They're reminded that "everyone's in this together." The ones who left aren't mentioned. Not in meetings, not in emails, not in watercooler chats. 

Their departure is a silence that spreads. A held breath. People stop talking about certain things. Stop asking questions. The ones who stay learn to self-censor. Learn to nod and move on.

Meanwhile, up the chain, decisions get made. People with titles and stock options discuss "synergy" and "disruption." They talk about "investing in the future." The future doesn't have a name. Doesn't have a face. Doesn't have a salary.

The ones who stay work longer hours. For less. They're told it's "temporary." That things will "get back to normal." Normal is a myth. Normal is what they had before the someone left. Before the layoffs. Before the "restructuring."

Their work goes on. The weight of it does too. In their shoulders, in their backs, in their tired eyes. They show up. They do their jobs. The weight accumulates.
